Indelve

Github | Pitch Deck
Pictures

This semester, as a part of Blockchain at Berkeley, I worked on an internal project team to develop Indelve, a blockhain-based research paper publishing application. It uses smart contracts to enable the entire process from uploading a paper to getting a paper read, rated, and voted on to having it published. What makes the system more unique than traditional publishing systems and research paper archives is that not only does the nature of blockchain allow for it to be a completely transparent process but also it allows for the public to partake in the publishing procedure as preliminary screeners to identify papers, through staking tokens of our internal cryptocurrency, that are of the highest quality to be reviewed first. The application is built with Solidity, React, and Web3.js. Please feel free to check it out in more details at the slide deck and repository linked above!

Pothole Detection (LA Hacks 2019)

Github | Devpost
Pictures

Potholes are a prevalent infrastructure problem, causing damage and accidents for drivers worldwide. Currently, there is no streamlined process to report potholes, a depression in the road caused by erosion, other than calling a number. However, it is unlikely that the average driver is going to immediately call this number while driving, remember the location, or report it afterwards. My team and I created an iOS app that functions as a dash cam and automates the process of reporting potholes. The app frequently sends image to a trained deep learning model on Google Cloud Platform. If it determines that the image contains a pothole, it sends the image and location to a Firebase database. An application developed using React and Javascript then visualizes the frequency of reported potholes on a heat map using Google Maps API and lists report details on a sidebar, information that can be used by infrastructure planners to analyze where potholes are causing the most problems.

Up 2 Date (Tree Hacks 2019)

Github | Devpost
Pictures

Current mainstream news outlet subscriptions generally only allow you to subscribe to perhaps one topic such as climate change or technology or only one news source. You wouldn't be able to subscribe to such a specific topic such as how technology is affecting climate change. My team and I developed a chrome extension that allows multi-source news subscription to user-generated topics. While reading a news article, a user may find a specific phrase or topic interesting such as "using technology to fight climate change". They can then choose to open the chrome extension, highlight this phrase, and subscribe to "using technology to fight climate change". Using a threshold algorithm by analyzing features such as news frequency, popularity, and volume published, if the topic is currently trending or there is relevant breaking news, the user is emailed an update as well as relevant news articles using the Microsoft Bing Search API. This was built using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Django, AWS, and Firebase. Built at Tree Hacks hacking under the awareness vertical.

Bac Trac (Cal Hacks 2018)

HackerEarth
Pictures

Currently, it is difficult to keep track of someone's exact blood alcohol content. People can only guess how much the alcohol is current affecting them. To address this and to promote safety, my team and I created an iOS app that uses personalized features including height, weight, and age to calculate a person's blood alcohol content based on how much they have had to drink. The user simply clicks a button every time a unit of drink is ingested and the app will update them on their blood alcohol content and how much longer they have left until they are completely sober. Additionally, the app features a system where friends can form groups before spending a night out so that they can monitor each other's levels and ensure that everyone is safe. This was built using Swift and Firebase.
